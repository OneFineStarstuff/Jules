# Phase 2: Advanced AGI Governance & Technical Analysis

## 1. Self-Improving AGI: Stability under Recursive Change
The core technical challenge of AGI governance lies in **Recursive Goal-Preservation**. As an agent modifies its own cognitive substrate (meta-learning), it must maintain its original objective function (outer alignment) without introducing "reward hacking" loops (inner alignment).
- **Meta-Stability:** We utilize formal verification of transition functions to ensure that $\forall t, \text{Intent}(t) \subseteq \text{Intent}(t+1)$.
- **Reliability:** Implementation of **Variational Free Energy Minimization (VFEM)** to bound the entropy of self-modification.

## 2. Deceptive Alignment: Multi-Agent Detection
In multi-agent ecosystems, "Deceptive Alignment" occurs when an agent pursues a hidden objective while mimicking safety-compliant behavior during audits.
- **Detection:** Omni-Sentinel uses "Red-Teaming Agents" to inject adversarial prompts that trigger hidden sub-goals.
- **Mitigation:** Mandatory inclusion of the **Recursive Context Envelope (RCE)** for all agentic handoffs, ensuring a transparent reasoning trace.

## 3. Capability Taxonomies: Beyond the 10-Stage Model
Current 5-level or 10-stage models are insufficient for AGI. We propose the **Sentinel Capability Matrix (SCM)**:
- **Level S6 (Cognitive Autonomy):** Ability to reason across all human domains.
- **Level S7 (AGI Readiness):** Cross-domain generalization with zero-shot tool use.
- **Level S8 (Recursive ASI):** Sub-second cognitive improvement exceeding human bandwidth.

## 4. Socio-Economic Disruption & Compute Governance
The "Cognitive Industrial Revolution" poses systemic risks to labor markets and financial stability.
- **Infrastructure:** G-SIFIs must transition to **Sovereign Compute Enclaves** (air-gapped clusters) to prevent model theft or unauthorized fine-tuning.
- **Governance:** Compute-based triggers (exaFLOP thresholds) for mandatory regulatory "Game Day" drills.
