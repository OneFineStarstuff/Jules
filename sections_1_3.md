# SECTION 1: AGI/ASI Governance Architecture for Board and Executive Leadership (2026–2030)

## 1.1 The "Homeostatic Enterprise" Framework
In the transition from narrow AI to Frontier AGI, traditional point-in-time audits are obsolete. Enterprise AGI Governance must be **homeostatic**—self-regulating systems that maintain operational equilibrium against capability drift.

### 1.1.1 Stakeholder Matrix & Mandates
- **Board of Directors**: Oversight of "Existential Enterprise Risk" (EER). Mandates a hard-wired IRMI (Internal Risk Mitigation Interface) kill-switch.
- **Chief Risk Officer (CRO)**: Governance of the "Probabilistic Balance Sheet." Focus on Model Risk Management (MRM) for AGI-driven credit and market exposure.
- **Chief AI Officer (CAIO)**: Orchestration of the "Cognitive Supply Chain." Accountability for agentic interoperability and alignment with corporate DNA.
- **CISO**: Transition from "Data Protection" to "Reasoning Protection." Focus on preventing model extraction, weights theft, and prompt injection at the agent-mesh level.

## 1.2 Capability Redlines & Tiered Oversight
Architectures must enforce capability thresholds. Crossing a redline (e.g., $10^{26}$ FLOP training, autonomous code-base mutation) triggers an immediate transition from Tier 2 (Standard Governance) to Tier 1 (Containment-First Governance).

# SECTION 2: Institutional-Grade AI Governance & Global Regulatory Enforcement

## 2.1 EU AI Act 2026+: The Enforcement Era
By 2026, enforcement of the EU AI Act necessitates a "Conformity-by-Design" approach for High-Risk AI and General-Purpose AI (GPAI) with systemic risk.
- **Transparency Mandates**: Automated generation of Technical Documentation (Annex IV) via Sentinel v2.4 telemetry.
- **Fundamental Rights Impact Assessment (FRIA)**: Integration of OPA-based checks into CI/CD to ensure no disparate impact on protected classes.

## 2.2 Global Standards Alignment (ISO/IEC 42001 & NIST AI RMF)
- **ISO/IEC 42001 (AIMS)**: Establishing the "Management Plane" for AI. Defining objectives, policies, and resource allocation for the AI lifecycle.
- **NIST AI RMF 2.0**: Operationalizing the "Map, Measure, Manage, Govern" functions into real-time observability dashboards.

# SECTION 3: Operational Integration: ISO 42001 AIMS and NIST AI RMF into CI/CD

## 3.1 Governance-as-Code (GaC)
Compliance is no longer a PDF; it is a Pull Request.
- **Telemetry Hooks**: Every AGI reasoning loop emits a "Governance Pulse" (TraceID + PolicyHash) to a WORM (Write-Once-Read-Many) Auditor.
- **Automated Conformity Assessments**: GitHub Actions trigger `sentinel-verify` commands that run adversarial red-teaming against model checkpoints before deployment to production clusters.
