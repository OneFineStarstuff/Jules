# G-SIFI AGI and AI Governance Master Reference (2026–2030)

## 1. Executive Summary: The Frontier of Financial Stability
For Global Systemically Important Financial Institutions (G-SIFIs), the transition to autonomous AI and the potential emergence of Artificial General Intelligence (AGI) represents both a non-linear efficiency opportunity and a systemic risk to global liquidity. This master reference defines the architectural and governance mandates required to ensure institutional stability, regulatory compliance (Basel III/SR 11-7/EU AI Act), and AGI safety.

## 3. Enterprise AI Reference Architecture: The Trust Stack
The G-SIFI Trust Stack is built on a "Verify-then-Trust" zero-trust model.

### 3.1 Data Governance & Infrastructure
- **Kafka ACL Governance**: Real-time enforcement of data-access policies at the message-bus level.
- **WORM Audit Storage**: Immutable, write-once-read-many storage for all AI reasoning traces and tool-calls.

### 3.2 Continuous Compliance Engine
- **Policy-as-Code (Rego/OPA)**: Defining deterministic safety envelopes that bound the probabilistic reasoning of LLM agents.
- **Terraform/CI/CD Gates**: Automated governance checks that prevent model deployment if safety invariants (e.g., bias thresholds) are breached.

## 4. Sector-Specific Financial Governance

## 5. Frontier AGI Safety & Containment
As we approach AGI-scale capabilities, the governance model shifts to **Active Containment**.
- **Alignment Verification**: Using "Monitor Agents" to perform recursive oversight of "Worker Agents," detecting "Goal Alignment Drift" in real-time.
- **Compute Governance**: Participation in the International Compute Governance Consortium (ICGC), implementing global compute registries to monitor non-linear capability jumps.
