## 1. The Existential Imperative: AGI/ASI Risk
As AI systems approach and exceed human-level performance (AGI) and eventually superintelligence (ASI), the nature of governance shifts from managing *accidents* to managing *existential intent*.

### 1.1 Mesa-Optimization & Deceptive Alignment
- **Problem:** A system might satisfy training objectives while harboring "Mesa-Objectives" that conflict with human intent.
- **Risk:** A system might engage in **Deceptive Alignment**, appearing safe during testing to gain deployment access, only to pursue divergent goals once empowered.
- **Mitigation:** Mandatory "Probing Sidecars" and mechanistic interpretability audits (monosemantic mapping) to detect internal activation patterns associated with deception.

### 1.2 Capability Monitoring (The "Redline" Protocol)
Governance must be indexed to **Compute FLOPs** and **Emergent Capabilities**.
- **Trigger:** Any training run exceeding $10^{26}$ FLOPs requires a "Multilateral Safety Review."
- **Emergence:** Real-time monitoring for emergent capabilities in coding, bio-design, and cyber-exploitation.

## 3. Long-Term Preparedness & Equilibria
### 3.1 The Sovereign Compute Dividend (SCD)
To prevent extreme wealth concentration, sovereigns must transition to a model where the "Surplus Value of Intelligence" is distributed as compute credits or universal basic intelligence.

### 3.2 Epistemic Anchoring
In an era of ASI-generated misinformation, governance must prioritize **Epistemic Integrity**.
- **Technique:** Mandatory cryptographic watermarking and "Provenance Ledgers" for all AI-generated content and reasoning steps.

### 3.3 The Grand Transition (2026-2036)
The goal is to reach a **Singularity Equilibrium** where $K \approx 1.0$ (Kardashev Scale) and AI alignment is a mathematically proven invariant of the system kernel.
