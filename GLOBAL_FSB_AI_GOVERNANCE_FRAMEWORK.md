# Global Governance Framework for High-Agency AI in G-SIFIs
**Authority:** Financial Stability Board (FSB) & Basel Committee on Banking Supervision (BCBS)
**Status:** Canonical Policy Blueprint
**Scope:** Global Systemically Important Financial Institutions & Critical Infrastructure
**Effective:** Phased implementation (2025–2030)

---

## 1. Supervisory Infrastructure: Digital Supervisor Nodes (DSN)
The FSB mandates the integration of **Digital Supervisor Nodes** directly into the compute kernels of G-SIFI agentic fabrics.
- **Real-Time Monitoring:** DSNs monitor high-frequency Autonomous Financial Agents (AFA) for "Herding Behaviors" and "Liquidity Spirals."
- **Federated Oversight:** Central Banks (ECB, PRA, Fed) maintain read-access to the **Epistemic Ledger** of their respective DSNs via mTLS-secured gRPC channels.
- **Intervention:** DSNs possess the technical authority to inject "Governance Latency" or execute an immediate "Hard Kill" (IRMI protocol) if systemic risk metrics are breached.

---

## 2. Legislative Model: Autonomous Financial Agent (AFA) Liability
We reject "Legal Personhood" for AI agents. Accountability must remain with the institutional legal entity.
- **Strict Liability:** Model developers and G-SIFIs are strictly liable for any cascading market failures or consumer harm generated by AFAs, regardless of the complexity of the agent's reasoning.
- **SMCR Integration:** Individual accountability (e.g., SMF24 in the UK) is extended to include the **Safety Certification** of the AFA's "Safety Constitution."

---

## 3. International Standards: Basel III & ISO 42001
- **Operational Risk:** AI-driven volatility is classified as a primary "Sovereign Operational Risk" under Basel III.
- **Capital Buffers:** Institutions must maintain an **Agentic Liquidity Buffer (ALB)** proportional to the exaFLOP-scale capability of their deployed AFA clusters.
- **ISO 42001 Harmony:** All G-SIFI AI management systems must be certified against ISO 42001, with specific Annex A controls mapped to SR 11-7 validation requirements.

---

## 5. Economic Transition: Deflation & Consolidation
- **Cost Reduction:** AFA deployment is projected to reduce middle-office costs by $60\%$.
- **Consolidation:** The FSB anticipates a wave of "Mega-Mergers" as G-SIFIs with superior AGI-alignment kernels gain insurmountable competitive advantages.
- **Redistribution:** Implementation of a "Stability Levy" on AFA-generated excess returns to fund global financial resilience initiatives.

---

## 6. Constitutional & Cultural Adaptation
- **Fiduciary Duty 2.0:** AI agents must be hard-coded with the **Fiduciary Invariant**: "The interest of the client and systemic stability shall supersede optimization of institutional P&L."
- **Citizen Assemblies:** G-SIFIs must submit their "Safety Constitutions" to periodic reviews by independent stakeholder assemblies to maintain democratic legitimacy.
