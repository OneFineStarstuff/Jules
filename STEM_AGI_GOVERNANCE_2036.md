# Phase 4: Long-Term Governance for STEM-AGI (2026–2036)

## 1. Technical Safety: Invariant-Preserving Self-Modification
As models transition to scientific superintelligence (STEM-AGI), the core risk is the loss of safety invariants during recursive self-optimization.
- **Glass-Box Hidden State Inspection:** Utilizing "Sparse Autoencoders for Scientific Reasoning" to map internal hidden states to human-understandable scientific axioms.
- **Constraint:** All self-modifying updates must be accompanied by a formal proof that the update preserves the "Non-Proliferation of Harm" invariant.

## 2. Macroeconomic Stabilization: Mitigating Cognitive Hollow-Out
The automation of white-collar scientific labor (STEM-hollowing) requires a new capital redistribution flow.
- **Cognitive Labor Dividend:** A levy on exaFLOP compute usage to fund the "Epistemic Retraining" of human scientists into high-fidelity oversight roles.
- **Design:** Capital flows move from "Model Owners" to "Sovereign Innovation Enclaves."

## 3. Treaty on Scientific Singularity (TSS)
A multilateral treaty focused on the safe ascent to superintelligence.
- **Compute Caps:** Static limits on training clusters (e.g., < 10^28 FLOPs) until safety parity is verified.
- **Mutual Inspection:** IAEA-style open-sky data center access for treaty signatories.
- **Safe Harbors:** Protected zones for human-only scientific discovery.

## 4. Game-Theoretic Analysis: Managed Pluralism
- **Winner-Take-All Risk:** The first nation to reach ASI gains an insurmountable lead.
- **Managed Pluralism Strategy:** Signatories agree to a "Compute Pool" where multiple aligned AGIs are trained with diverse objective functions. This enforces a pluralistic equilibrium, preventing any single agent from becoming a "Singleton" with misaligned goals.
