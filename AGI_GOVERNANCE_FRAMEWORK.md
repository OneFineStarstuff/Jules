# AGI/ASI Governance Framework for the Global 2000

## 1. Governance Accountability Model
Effective AGI governance requires a shift from human-only oversight to machine-augmented accountability structures.

### 1.1 The Chief Cognitive Orchestrator (CCO)
- **Role**: C-suite executive responsible for the "Intelligence Risk Appetite" of the organization.
- **Mandate**: Balancing agentic autonomy with institutional stability and regulatory constraints.

### 1.2 Auditor-Agent Swarms
- **Mechanism**: Deploying specialized, independent monitoring agents that analyze the "Reasoning Traces" of production worker agents in real-time.
- **Goal-Alignment Verification**: Continuous verification that agent intents remain within the "Institutional Alignment Space."

## 2. Risk Management & Containment
### 2.1 Capability Monitoring
- **Thresholds**: Board-approved "Redlines" for non-linear capability jumps (e.g., autonomous code synthesis or unauthorized substrate expansion).
- **Compute Governance**: Hardware-level monitoring of TFLOPS allocation to detect "Shadow Training" runs.

### 2.2 Alignment Verification Protocols
- **Cognitive Resonance Checks**: Periodic challenges to agents to ensure goal stability across long horizons.
- **Formal Verification**: Mathematical proofs of safety for critical sub-components of autonomous trading and risk engines.
