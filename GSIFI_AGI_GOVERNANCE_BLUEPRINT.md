# G-SIFI AGI/ASI and AI Governance Master Reference (2026–2030)

## 1. Governance Pillars & Accountability Structures

Effective governance of AGI requires a shift from human-only oversight to a multilayered, machine-augmented pillar model.

### 1. Pillar 1: Accountability & Roles
- **Chief AI Governance Officer (CAIGO)**: Board-level role responsible for the "Intelligence Risk Appetite."
- **Sentinel Governance Operator**: Engineering role managing the GDL rule-sets and hardware kill-switches.
- **Auditor-Agent Swarms**: Autonomous monitoring agents with read-only access to the WORM audit fabric, providing real-time compliance reporting.

### 2. Pillar 2: Policy & Risk Infrastructure
- **Sentinel AI Risk Engine**: Evaluates "Epistemic Uncertainty" and "Goal Alignment Score" for frontier models.
- **Minimum Viable AI Governance Stack (MVGS)**: An air-gapped, sovereign unit for critical incident containment.

### 3. Pillar 3: AI-Ready Data & Observability
- **Kafka ACL Governance**: Real-time enforcement of data-access policies at the message-bus level.
- **WORM Audit Fabric**: Immutable, write-once-read-many storage for reasoning traces.

## 3. Enterprise AI Reference Architecture: The Trust Stack

The architecture follows a **Zero-Trust for Agents** model, separating the "Intelligence Substrate" from the "Governance Control Plane."

- **Model Registry**: Centralized repository for "Approved-for-Production" weights and prompt templates.
- **Policy Engine (OPA)**: Sidecar-based enforcement of Rego policies (Policy-as-Code).
- **CI/CD Governance Gates**: Prevent deployment of models lacking a "Compliance Passport" or failing formal verification checks.
