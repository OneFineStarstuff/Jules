## 1. Self-Improving AGI Systems: Stability & Containment
### 1.1 Recursive Self-Improvement (RSI)
RSI occurs when an AI system utilizes its reasoning capacity to optimize its own architecture or weight configuration.
*   **Stability Challenge:** Continuous optimization loops create a risk of **Recursive Collapse**, where the goal-vector $g(t)$ deviates from the safety manifold faster than human oversight can intervene.
*   **Containment:** Requires the implementation of **Control-theoretic Stability Loops** that monitor the Lyapunov stability of the model's internal objective function.
