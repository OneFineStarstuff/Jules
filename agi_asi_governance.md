# Enterprise AGI & Potential ASI Governance Framework

## 1. Capability Monitoring & Compute Governance
As systems approach AGI capabilities, the governance model shifts from "Output Monitoring" to "Resource Containment."

- **Compute Quotas**: Hard-coded FLOP limits for specific agent swarms to prevent uncontrolled capability jumps.
- **Hardware Kill-Switches**: Integration with secure enclaves (TEE) that can freeze model weights if redline capabilities (e.g., self-exfiltration logic) are detected.

## 2. Containment Protocols for Self-Multiplying Systems
Self-multiplying systems (agents that can spawn sub-agents) require **Recursive Containment**.

- **Quota Inheritance**: Sub-agents inherit a strictly decreasing subset of the parent's compute and financial quotas.
- **Recursive Alignment Verification**: Every generation of spawned agents must pass a "Stability Accord" check against the Sentinel Platform before activation.

## 3. Executive Role: The Chief Cognitive Orchestrator (CCO)
The CCO manages the "Cognitive Equilibrium" of the enterprise.
- **Responsibility**: Balancing human capital with autonomous agent leverage.
- **Mandate**: Ensuring that the AGI/ASI transition aligns with long-term enterprise values and regulatory safety redlines.
