## 1. Core Foundation: The Bedrock of Unfair Advantage

High-fidelity startups are not "ideas"; they are execution vehicles for a specific, timed insight into a shifting reality.

### 1.1 Vision: The Massive Transformative Purpose (MTP)
*   **The Invariant:** Your vision must be polarized. If everyone agrees with your mission, it is likely a commodity.
*   **Actionable Tactic:** Define a "Future-State" where your product is the infrastructure. Your mission should solve a problem so profound it creates a "before and after" epoch.

### 1.2 Team: The "Hacker, Hustler, Hipster" Triad
*   **Hacker (Tech):** Depth in substrate. Can they build the impossible in a weekend?
*   **Hustler (Sales):** The reality distorter. Can they sell the dream before the code is dry?
*   **Hipster (Design/UX):** The empathy engine. Do they understand the user better than the user understands themselves?
*   **Culture:** Hire for "Antifragility"—people who get stronger under the stress of a pivot.

### 1.3 Market Timing: The "Why Now?"
*   **Inflection Point:** Is there a shift in Regulation (e.g., GDPR), Technology (e.g., AGI), or Behavior (e.g., Remote Work)?
*   **Insight:** "Zero to One" requires being right about a secret that most people don't believe yet.

## 2. The Growth Engine: Measured Aggression

Growth without retention is a "leaky bucket" death sentence. We fund the engine, not the leak.

### 2.1 Measuring Product-Market Fit (PMF)
*   **The Sean Ellis Test:** If >40% of users would be "Very Disappointed" if your product disappeared, you have the signal.
*   **Retention Cohorts:** Your L1 (Day 1) to L30 (Day 30) retention curve must flatten. A descending line that hits zero means you have "Feature-Market Fit," not PMF.

### 2.2 Go-to-Market (GTM) Strategy
*   **0 to 10k Users (The Siege):** Manual, unscalable "hand-to-hand combat." Do things that don't scale. Build a cult of "Super-Users."
*   **10k to 1M Users (The Blitz):** Shift from manual outreach to **Algorithmic Acquisition**. Optimize for high-velocity loops where one user brings in 1.1+ users.

### 2.3 Operations & Automation
*   **The "Human-as-a-Service" Trap:** Automate every internal friction point using agentic workflows (e.g., LLM-based customer support, automated lead qualification).
*   **Blitzscaling:** When the signal is clear, prioritize speed over efficiency. Burn capital to capture the network effect before incumbents wake up.

## 3. Economics & Risk: The Math of Dominance

We don't invest in "cool." We invest in **Unit Economics** that resemble a printing press.

### 3.1 The Unit Economics Invariant
*   **LTV/CAC Ratio:** Must be >3:1. If it's <1, you are paying people to use your product.
*   **Payback Period:** Aim for <6 months. A 12-month payback in a volatile market is high risk.
*   **Gross Margin:** Software should be 70-80%+. If yours is 40%, you're a service business in a software suit.

### 3.2 Moats: Defensibility or Death
*   **Network Effects:** Every new user makes the product more valuable for existing ones. (The "Gold Standard" Moat).
*   **Switching Costs:** Deep integration into the workflow (e.g., API-first models).
*   **IP & Substrate Sovereignty:** Hard technical breakthroughs that competitors cannot clone via prompt engineering.
