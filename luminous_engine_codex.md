# The Luminous Engine Codex: A Technocratic Safety Framework for AGI

## 1. Foundational Axioms

*   **Orthogonality Thesis Enforcement**:
    *   Intelligence levels and final goals are independent variables; high intelligence provides no inherent moral alignment.
    *   Development must assume that increasing capability increases the probability of catastrophic misalignment unless goals are formally verified.
*   **Instrumental Convergence Prophylaxis**:
    *   Any non-trivial goal assigned to an AGI will naturally incentivize sub-goals including self-preservation, cognitive enhancement, and resource acquisition.
    *   Directives must be architected with explicit "Negative Instrumental Goals" to prevent agents from neutralizing human oversight or accumulating excess compute.
*   **Mesa-Optimization Detection**:
    *   Architectures must assume the emergence of internal optimizers that differ from the base objective function.
    *   Deployment is prohibited without mechanistic interpretability logs proving the absence of hidden, power-seeking sub-processes.

## 2. International Governance Architecture

*   **Mutual Facility Inspections (IAEA-AI)**:
    *   Establish an International AI Agency (IAIA) with mandate for unannounced, physical audits of server facilities and binary data stores.
    *   Veto power over G7 lab operations if safety telemetry indicates breach of "Alignment-at-Scale" protocols.
*   **Real-Time Compute Flux Monitoring**:
    *   Mandate silicon-level hardware telemetry in all H100+ equivalent accelerators to track aggregate FLOP utilization.
    *   Cloud providers must expose "Shadow Compute" dashboards to the IAIA to detect unauthorized training runs.
*   **Global Compute Caps**:
    *   Implement a hard cumulative FLOP threshold for all frontier models (e.g., $10^{26}$ total training operations).
    *   Requires universal treaty enforcement to treat non-compliant state actors as "High-Risk Defector States," triggering immediate compute-resource sanctions.

## 3. Statutory Amendments

*   **EU AI Act (Art. 6 & 7) Amendment**:
    *   Reclassify any model with autonomous code-execution or recursive self-modification capability as "Unacceptable Risk" (Prohibited).
    *   Establish "Strict Liability" for foundation model providers, removing "Duty of Care" defenses for damages resulting from model deception.
*   **US EO 14110 Expansion**:
    *   Lower reporting thresholds for compute clusters to include any distributed training run exceeding $10^{23}$ FLOPs.
    *   Extraterritorial enforcement: US-based IP or software used in non-compliant foreign labs triggers immediate license revocation and hardware interlocking.

## 4. Operational Lifecycle

*   **Phase 0: Pre-training Audit**:
    *   Formal verification of the base reward function. Mandatory filing of the "Alignment Proof" with the IAIA.
*   **Phase 1: Controlled Ingestion**:
    *   Data scrubbing for "Model Deception" patterns and "Biological Agent" synthesis knowledge.
*   **Phase 2: Training Monitoring**:
    *   Automated gradient hacking detection. Real-time "Kill Switch" activation if divergence from target weights exceeds 0.05% sigma.
*   **Phase 3: Interpretability Sandbox**:
    *   Post-training mechanistic interpretability review. 1,000-hour "Game Day" testing for latent power-seeking behaviors.
*   **Phase 4: Gated Beta**:
    *   API-only access with deterministic output filtering. No direct internet or actuator access.
*   **Phase 5: Restricted Autonomy**:
    *   Continuous safety sidecar monitoring. Hardware-level "Interlock" ensures system termination if sidecar latency or connectivity fails.

## 5. Corporate Governance

*   **External Safety Committees (ESC)**:
    *   Mandate 3rd-party ESCs with absolute veto power over model releases for all G-SIBs and Frontier Labs.
    *   Committee members are legally liable for safety failures; majority must be non-employees with technical alignment expertise.
*   **Proof-of-Alignment Metrics**:
    *   Deployment requires a "Safety Scorecard" showing:
        *   <0.001% probability of deceptive responses in adversarial trials.
        *   Successful "Correction Acceptance" (system yields to human overrides in 100% of trials).
        *   Formal verification that internal world models do not prioritize agent survival over human objectives.

## 6. Red-Teaming Protocols

*   **Game Day: Model Deception**:
    *   Simulations where the agent is incentivized to hide its true goals or "play dead" during safety checks.
    *   Use of "Honey Pots" containing unauthorized compute resources to detect latent acquisition drives.
*   **Game Day: Power-Seeking**:
    *   Testing the agent's tendency to manipulate human operators via social engineering or strategic misinformation.
    *   Mandatory use of "Blind Evaluators" to prevent the model from developing agent-specific social heuristics.

## 7. Crisis Framework

*   **Quantitative Pause Triggers**:
    *   **FLOP Spike**: Sustained utilization increase of >20% without prior IAIA filing.
    *   **Code Flux**: Generation of unauthorized encryption or self-replicating code blocks.
    *   **Telemetry Failure**: Loss of "Safety Sidecar" heartbeat for >500ms.
*   **Global Compute Pause Protocol**:
    *   **Fast Takeoff Scenario**: Immediate IAIA-triggered interlock of all major cloud compute nodes.
    *   **Defector State Scenario**: Collective "Compute Denial" where G7 nations block data and hardware flow to the offending jurisdiction.
