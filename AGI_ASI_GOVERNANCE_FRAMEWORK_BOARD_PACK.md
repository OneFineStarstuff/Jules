## 2. Technical Containment & Control Architecture
For systems exhibiting self-multiplying or self-improving behaviors:

- **IRMI (Inherent Risk Mitigation Interface):** A mandatory silicon-level safety interlock. If model weights deviate from "Safe Activation Regions," the IRMI sever power to the GPU clusters (0.5ms latency).
- **Containment Kernels:** AGI logic MUST be executed within a "Software-Defined Sandbox" that intercepts all system calls, network egress, and disk writes for Sentinel-based policy validation.
- **Epistemic Anchor Protocols:** Preventing "Model Collapse" and "Deception" by anchoring reasoning steps to an immutable WORM ledger (Kafka).

2. **System:** The integration environment and hardware substrate.

12. **Objective/Reward Function:** Alignment with the Corporate Goal Function.
13. **Controller:** The GDL-based enforcement kernel.
14. **Knowledge Base:** The "Epistemic Grounding" layer.
15. **Memory:** Recursive Context Envelopes and state logs.
16. **Policy:** Rego-based safety and compliance rules.
17. **Evaluation Metrics:** Real-time risk and deception scores.
18. **Safety Layer:** Hardware-level IRMI and software circuit breakers.

## 6. AGI Governance Glossary & Maturity Rubric

### 6.1 Glossary of Terms
- **MCoI (Marginal Cost of Intelligence):** The incremental cost to generate a unit of autonomous reasoning.
- **Epistemic Anchoring:** The process of verifying an agent's reasoning against a "Golden Source" of verified truth.
- **Reasoning Trace:** A non-repudiable log of every sub-task, tool-call, and inference step in an agentic chain.
- **GDL (Governance Description Language):** A formal language (EBNF-compliant) for defining immutable safety invariants.
- **IRMI (Inherent Risk Mitigation Interface):** A hardware-level circuit breaker for compute clusters.
