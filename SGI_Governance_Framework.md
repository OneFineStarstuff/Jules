# Scientific General Intelligence (SGI) Governance & Deployment Framework

## 1. Architectural Invariants
SGI systems are architected for **Epistemic Substantiation**. Unlike probabilistic LLMs, SGI necessitates:
*   **Symbolic Reasoning Layer:** Formal verification of mathematical and physical derivations.
*   **Causal Inference Engine:** Mapping correlations to physical mechanisms to prevent "hallucinated science."
*   **Veridical Grounding:** Mandatory citation of cryptographically signed experimental data nodes.

## 2. Governance Gating
*   **Proof-of-Alignment (PoA):** Models must emit a formal safety proof for any proposed physical experiment before compute is allocated.
*   **Epistemic Thresholding:** Inferences with a confidence-to-veracity ratio below 0.95 are automatically routed to human-in-the-loop (HITL) doctoral-level review.

## 3. High-Stakes Deployment (Near-AGI)
*   **Sandbox Isolation:** Kubernetes-based ephemeral pods with Zero-Trust network egress.
*   **Substrate Control:** Integration with the **IRMI (Inherent Risk Mitigation Interface)** for kernel-level halts on goal-drift detection.
*   **Auditability:** Immutable RCE (Recursive Context Envelope) logging of every reasoning step in the hypothesis generation chain.
