# G-SIFI AGI/ASI Governance: Technical Architectures and Global Frameworks (2026–2030)

## 1. Executive Summary: The Intelligence Sovereignty Mandate
For Global Systemically Important Financial Institutions (G-SIFIs), the transition from narrow AI to Artificial General Intelligence (AGI) represents the most significant shift in operational risk since the advent of electronic trading. This report defines a high-assurance governance architecture designed to reconcile the performance requirements of autonomous agentic swarms with the rigorous stability mandates of global financial regulators (Basel III, SR 11-7, EU AI Act).

## 2. Multilayered AI Governance Pillars
A robust institutional framework must be built on five primary pillars:

### 2.1 Accountability & Roles
- **Chief Cognitive Orchestrator (CCO)**: A C-suite role managing the "Intelligence Risk Appetite" and cross-functional agentic workflows.
- **Sentinel Governance Operators**: Specialized engineers responsible for the maintenance of GDL (Governance Description Language) rule-sets and hardware-level kill-switches.

### 2.2 Policy & Risk Infrastructure
- **Sentinel AI Risk Engine**: A real-time telemetry processor evaluating "Epistemic Uncertainty" and "Goal Alignment" for every agentic transaction.
- **Minimum Viable AI Governance Stack (MVGS)**: An air-gapped governance unit for critical incident containment.

### 2.3 AI-Ready Data & Monitoring
- **Kafka-based WORM Audit Logging**: Every reasoning "thought" and tool-call is streamed to a Write-Once-Read-Many (WORM) Kafka fabric for forensic non-repudiation and regulatory discovery.
- **Centralized Logging & Observability**: Utilization of OpenTelemetry (OTEL) with agent-specific semantic conventions (e.g., `agent.reasoning.path`, `agent.goal.alignment_score`).
